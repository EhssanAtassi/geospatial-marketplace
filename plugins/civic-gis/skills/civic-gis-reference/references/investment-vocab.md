# Investment Vocabulary

Economic-development terminology investors use, and what small-town economic-development officials need to know to be credible. Reference for `civic-gis:investment-profile` and `civic-gis:investment-sites`.

## Investor's Five Questions

Every site-selection conversation goes through these in order:

1. **Who lives there?** Demographics, labor force, education, language.
2. **How do I get to/from?** Highway access, port/airport/rail, broadband, public transit.
3. **What's already there?** Existing industries, anchor employers, supplier network, complementary businesses.
4. **Where can I build?** Available zoned land, parcel sizes, average prices, utility access.
5. **Why now?** Recent infrastructure improvements, incentives, market timing.

A town's investment profile that doesn't answer all five gets discarded.

## Incentive Types

### Tax-based

| Term | Definition | Typical magnitude |
|---|---|---|
| **TIF** (Tax Increment Financing) | Use future tax increase to fund infrastructure now | Multi-year, can fund $1M-$100M+ projects |
| **Property tax abatement** | Reduced property tax for N years | 50-100% for 5-20 years |
| **Sales tax exemption** | Equipment purchases, construction materials | 100% on qualifying items |
| **Income tax credit** | Per-job or per-investment | $1k-$10k per job typical |
| **Tax holiday** | Full tax exemption period | 5-10 years common in MENA / Asia |
| **Opportunity Zone** (US) | Federal program; capital gains tax benefits for low-income census tracts | Significant for long-hold investments |
| **Enterprise Zone** (varies) | State/local equivalents | Region-specific |
| **Special Economic Zone** (international) | Industrial parks with reduced regulation | Common in MENA, Asia, Africa |

### Cash / grant-based

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Cash grant** | Direct payment to the investor |
| **Performance grant** | Paid out as job/investment milestones hit |
| **Forgivable loan** | Loan that converts to grant if milestones met |
| **Workforce training grant** | Pays to train new hires |
| **Relocation grant** | Pays moving costs |
| **Infrastructure grant** | Town pays for road/utility extensions to site |

### In-kind

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Land subsidy** | Town transfers land at below-market price |
| **Free infrastructure** | Roads, utilities to site at no cost |
| **Permit fast-track** | Guaranteed processing time |
| **Single point of contact** | One government employee handles all approvals |
| **One-stop-shop** | Single office handles every permit type |

## Industry-Specific Vocabulary

### Manufacturing

- **Greenfield** — new construction on undeveloped land
- **Brownfield** — redevelopment of contaminated former industrial site (often comes with cleanup obligations and tax credits)
- **3-phase power** — required for industrial machinery
- **Heavy load** — utility / road capacity for trucks, tankers
- **Just-in-time (JIT)** — supplier proximity matters for low-inventory ops
- **Cluster** — concentration of related industries (auto, aerospace, electronics)

### Logistics / Warehousing

- **Cross-docking** — short-stay warehouse, products move through fast
- **Intermodal** — connects rail + truck + port
- **Last-mile** — final delivery; needs urban-edge sites with road access
- **FTZ** (Foreign-Trade Zone, US) — defer/avoid customs duties

### Hospitality

- **Daily Average Rate (ADR)** — revenue per occupied room per night
- **RevPAR** (Revenue per Available Room) — combines occupancy + ADR
- **Feeder market** — where guests come from
- **Anchor attraction** — landmark that drives visits

### Agriculture / Agribusiness

- **Acreage** — land area (US)
- **Hectare** — land area (metric, 10,000 m²)
- **Irrigation pressure** — water rights, supply reliability
- **Cold chain** — refrigerated logistics for produce
- **Value-added processing** — turn raw crop into shelf-ready goods (canning, milling, packaging)

### Tech / Services

- **Talent density** — skilled workers per capita
- **Class A office** — top-tier modern building (HVAC, fiber, parking)
- **Coworking** — shared flexible office
- **Time zone advantage** — coverage of US East + EU + Asia hours

## Common Investor Asks

Investors typically need these documents to make a site decision. The investment-profile output should anticipate them:

1. **Town profile / fact sheet** (this is what investment-profile generates)
2. **Site portfolio** with at least 3-5 candidate parcels — investment-sites
3. **Workforce study** — labor pool, wages, skills, training providers
4. **Utility cost estimates** — power, water, gas, internet
5. **Logistics cost analysis** — distance/cost to suppliers, customers, ports
6. **Tax + incentive estimate** — pro-forma after-tax IRR
7. **Permits timeline** — best/worst case for project to break ground
8. **Comparable cities analysis** — what similar projects went where, why

## Trust Signals

What makes an investment profile *credible* rather than promotional:

- **Sourcing on every stat** — investors verify numbers; missing sources kill deals.
- **Recency** — anything older than 3 years gets discounted; over 5 years dismissed.
- **Specificity** — "5,000 m² industrial plot at $50/m² with 3-phase power at the curb" beats "available industrial land."
- **Risks acknowledged** — leaving out known risks (currency, regulatory uncertainty, infrastructure gaps) makes investors distrust the whole document.
- **References** — names of recent investors (with permission), contact info for chambers of commerce, banking partners.
- **No hyperbole** — "the next Dubai" claims hurt; concrete differentiators help.

## Small-Town Specific Strategy

Small cities and towns compete against major metros for investment. Common winning angles:

1. **Cost differential** — labor, real estate, utilities significantly cheaper than nearby metro.
2. **Quality of life** — schools, safety, commute time, environmental quality.
3. **Niche specialization** — *the* place for a specific industry (auto-supply, agritech, eldercare, etc.).
4. **Speed of decision-making** — small-town governments can approve faster than big-city bureaucracies.
5. **Talent retention** — local college / training facility producing graduates who stay.
6. **Public-private alignment** — the mayor + chamber + utility + banks all coordinate.

`civic-gis:investment-profile` should surface whichever of these angles is strongest for the given town.

## Post-Conflict Context

In reconstruction settings (Syria, Iraq, Lebanon, etc.), investors have additional concerns:

- **Property title clarity** — can the investor get clean title, or are there competing claims from displaced persons?
- **Currency convertibility** — can profits be repatriated?
- **Sanctions exposure** — are partners, banks, suppliers on any sanctions list?
- **Physical security** — current and projected
- **Insurance availability** — political risk insurance (MIGA), war risk
- **Reconstruction-specific funds** — World Bank, EBRD, regional development banks may co-finance

`investment-profile` for reconstruction-context towns must address these explicitly.
